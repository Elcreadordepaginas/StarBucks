50 Years Of Serving Communities

When the first Starbucks® store opened in 1971, our company was that only store in Seattle's historic Pike Place Market. From a narrow counter, Starbucks® offered some of the best bean coffee in the world. The name, inspired by Moby Dick, evoked the romance of the high seas and the seafaring tradition of the first coffee merchants.

In 1981, Howard Schultz (former president and CEO of Starbucks®) walked into a Starbucks® store for the first time. From his first cup of Sumatra, Howard was strongly drawn to Starbucks® and joined a year later. In 1983, Howard traveled to Italy and became captivated by Italian coffee shops and the romance of the coffee drinking experience. He had the vision of bringing that tradition of Italian coffees to the United States. I wanted to create a place for conversation and a sense of community, a third place between work and home. He left the company for a short period of time to start his own Is Giornale coffee shops and returned in August 1987 to purchase Starbucks® with the help of local investors.
